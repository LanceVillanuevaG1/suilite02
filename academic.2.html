About

Academic burnout- it is a state of emotional, mental, and physical exhaustion resulting from prolonged stress and pressure related to academic

Causes of Academic Burnout:

 

1. Overload of Work: Too much coursework, assignments, and exams can overwhelm students. 

2. High Expectations: Pressure to excel academically or meet unrealistic standards can contribute to burnout. 

3. Lack of Support: Inadequate support from teachers, peers, or family members can exacerbate burnout. 

4. Perfectionism: Striving for perfection in  academics can lead to stress and burnout.

So, why does academic burnout happen?

 "Well, too much homework and high expectations can overwhelm us. But there are ways to avoid burnout! Taking breaks, getting enough sleep, and asking for help when we need it can make a big difference. When we take care of ourselves, we feel better, do better in school, and keep enjoying learning.

Let's remember that school is important, but so is our well-being. So, let's work smart, take breaks, and support each other to avoid academic burnout."

-Am-is, Elieza
