About

Physical bullying- is the use of physical force, such as hitting, kicking, or pushing, to harm or intimidate another person. Certainly, here's a breakdown of the causes and effects of physical bullying:

Causes: 1. Power Imbalance- Bullies may perceive themselves as stronger or more dominant than their victims, leading to acts of physical aggression. 2. Lack of Empathy- Some individuals engage in physical bullying due to a lack of empathy or understanding of the impact of their actions on others.

We need to work together to stop physical bullying in our communities. It hurts people physically and emotionally. By being kind, showing respect, and having strong rules against bullying, we can make sure everyone feels safe and valued. Let's speak up, take action, and make our world a better, kinder place without bullying." -Am-is, Elieza

 

 

Effects: 1. Physical Injuries - Victims of physical bullying often suffer from bruises, cuts, sprains, or fractures, requiring medical attention. 2. Emotional Impact- Physical bullying can lead to feelings of fear, anxiety, helplessness, and low self-esteem in victims, contributing to mental health issues such as depression and PTSD. 3. Long-Term Trauma- The effects of physical bullying can extend into adulthood, affecting the victim's ability to trust others, form healthy relationships, and cope with stress.
