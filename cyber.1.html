Cyberbullying- is the use of digital technology, such as the internet and social media, to harass, intimidate, or harm others through malicious messages, posts, or actions.

Causes:  

 Effects of Preventing Cyberbullying:

POWER IMBALANCE- Bullies may perceive themselves as stronger or more dominant than their victims, leading to acts of physical aggression.  

    EMOTIONAL WELL-BEING- Prevention efforts reduce the emotional distress and psychological harm experienced by victims of cyberbullying.

 

             LACK OF EMPATHY- Some individuals engage in physical bullying due to a lack of empathy or understanding of the impact of their actions on others.

 ONLINE SAFETY- Educating individuals about cyberbullying prevention promotes safer online interactions and digital citizenship.

Why does cyberbullying happen? 

Sometimes, it's because people can hide behind screens and say mean things without getting caught. Other times, it's because they want to get back at someone or they're pressured by friends. But we can stop cyberbullying! By being kind online, standing up for each other, and reporting bullying when we see it, we can make the internet a safer and happier place. Let's remember that our words and actions online affect real people, and we should always treat others with respect and empathy. Together, we can prevent cyberbullying and make the online world a better place for everyone." - Am-is, Elieza
