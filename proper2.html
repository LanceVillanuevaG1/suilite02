Proper waste disposal-    is the safe and responsible management f waste materials to prevent harm to human health and the environment.

Causes of Improper Waste Disposal       1. Lack of Awareness: People may not be aware of the potential harm caused by improper waste disposal. 2. Inadequate Infrastructure: Some areas lack proper waste disposal facilities, leading to improper dumping of waste. 3. Irresponsible Behavior: Individuals may choose convenience over proper disposal methods, such as littering or illegal dumping.

Effects of Proper Waste Disposal: 1. Environmental Protection: Proper waste disposal helps protect ecosystems, water sources, and air quality. 2. Public Health: It reduces the risk of diseases and pollution-related health issues in communities. 3. Resource Conservation: Recycling and proper disposal conserve valuable resources for future generations.

Why does improper waste disposal happen? Sometimes, it's because we don't know better, or we don't have good places to put our trash. Other times, it's just because we're not careful enough.
